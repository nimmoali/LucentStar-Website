Why people leave Taplio

The pricing maths. Taplio's advertised entry plan does not include AI features. To use AI writing you need the mid tier at roughly $65 per month, which comes with a credit allowance that active creators can exhaust in a couple of weeks.

Account safety. Some of Taplio's features have relied on a Chrome extension and cookie-based authentication. Those are exactly the access patterns LinkedIn targeted in its enforcement action against third-party tools, and reviewers now treat access method as a primary buying criterion.

Generic output. Prompt-driven AI writing converges on the same LinkedIn house style. If your posts could carry anyone's name, they are not building your authority.

How we chose

  • Voice: does the tool learn how you write, or just generate?
  • Safety: how does it touch your LinkedIn account, if at all?
  • Quality control: does anything stop a weak post going out?
  • Honest pricing: what do you actually pay to get the useful features?

The 8 best Taplio alternatives in 2026

1.LucentSignal

Best for sounding like yourself

Disclosure: LucentSignal is our product. We have listed real limitations below so you can judge it fairly.

LucentSignal is a LinkedIn writing workflow built around one idea: the post should sound like you. A Ghostwriter profile learns your cadence and vocabulary from writing examples and voice rules, three writing styles (Thoughtful, Focused and Challenging) shape how the argument is built, and every post is scored out of 40 across hook, insight, clarity and closing before you save it, with specific feedback rather than a vanity number.

It never connects to your LinkedIn account. No Chrome extension, no cookie access, no automation. You plan and score posts, then export to Hootsuite, Agorapulse, Airtable, Notion or Google Calendar. That makes it the lowest-risk option on this list by design.

Pros

  • Ghostwriter voice matching included on the free plan
  • Quality scoring with written feedback on every post
  • Zero account risk: exports instead of connecting
  • Honest pricing, no credit system

Cons

  • No direct publishing to LinkedIn yet
  • No engagement analytics
  • Lower post volumes than bulk-generation tools

Free: 3 posts, no card. Creator £19/mo (20 posts). Pro £39/mo (75 posts). Try it free

2.Supergrow

Best budget option

Supergrow has built its reputation on generous AI writing limits at a low price, with voice training, carousel creation and scheduling included. If Taplio's credit system is what pushed you away, this is the most direct answer to that specific complaint.

Pros

  • Strong value at around $19/mo
  • Voice training and carousel tools included
  • No restrictive credit ceiling at entry level

Cons

  • No quality gate; volume can outrun standards
  • Output still needs editing to avoid the generic feel

From ~$19/mo. Free trial available.

3.MagicPost

Best for safe direct publishing

MagicPost connects through LinkedIn's official API as a verified partner, which makes it the standout choice if you want one-click publishing without the extension risk. It generates posts from ideas, articles and YouTube videos, and learns your style over time.

Pros

  • Official LinkedIn API partner, low account risk
  • Good content repurposing from video and articles
  • Clean, focused interface

Cons

  • Style learning is lighter than a full voice profile
  • Fewer planning features than workflow tools

From ~$39/mo. Check current plans.

4.Kleo

Best all-in-one brand system

Kleo positions itself as a complete personal brand system with voice learning, a knowledge base that improves output over time, and no credit ceilings. It is one of the more capable tools here, priced accordingly.

Pros

  • Voice learning plus a knowledge base
  • No credit limits
  • Broad feature set in one subscription

Cons

  • At ~$99/mo it is the priciest option here
  • More tool than most solo creators need

~$99/mo. Confirm current pricing and LinkedIn access method.

5.ContentIn

Best for templates and planning

ContentIn pairs an AI ghostwriter with a large template library and weekly planning, aimed at people who want a repeatable system rather than one-off generation. Migration from Taplio is straightforward.

Pros

  • Strong template library and idea management
  • Plan a week of content quickly
  • LinkedIn-first, trained on LinkedIn content

Cons

  • Templates can homogenise output if leaned on heavily
  • AI generation limits vary by plan

From $15/mo (Essentials). Free trial available.

6.EasyGen

Best for hook-driven posts

EasyGen generates posts using patterns drawn from high-performing creators, with style customisation, topic ideas and voice-note dictation. It is built for engagement-first writing, which is either exactly what you want or exactly what you are trying to escape.

Pros

  • Trained on what performs, strong hooks
  • Voice-note capture for ideas on the go
  • Simple to use

Cons

  • Performance patterns can read as formulaic
  • Less depth for long-form argument building

From $49/mo (Basic). See their site for current pricing.

7.AuthoredUp

Best if you write it yourself

AuthoredUp deliberately does not write for you. It is a formatting, preview and analytics layer for posts you author yourself, using LinkedIn's official API. If your problem with Taplio was the AI rather than the workflow, this is the principled alternative.

Pros

  • Excellent formatting and post previews
  • Deep analytics on your own content
  • Official API, account-safe

Cons

  • No drafting help at all
  • You still face the blank page

From $19.95/mo (Individual). Free trial available.

8.Scripe

Best for voice and video input

Scripe turns voice notes, videos and meetings into LinkedIn posts, learning from what you feed it. It suits people who think out loud and want their spoken ideas converted into written content with minimal typing.

Pros

  • Voice and video to post is genuinely useful
  • Learns from your real spoken ideas
  • Good for consistent output from busy schedules

Cons

  • Spoken-to-written conversion still needs editing
  • Pricier than most tools on this list at solo level

From €55/mo billed yearly (Solo). Trial available.
